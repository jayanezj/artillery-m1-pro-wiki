# The PTFE Tube
This tube guides the filament from the spool to the extruder.

## Required Parts
<table>
<tr>
  <th>Part</th>
  <th>Image</th>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

- **One-way connector:** This connector prevents the tube from moving outward from the extruder.
  - You can reuse the one from the original PTFE tube.
  - Replacement connectors are included in the [Artillery kit](https://artillery3d.com/products/ptfe-tube-m1).
  - You can get a compatible connector on [Aliexpress (ptfe embedded bowden collect)](https://aliexpress.com/item/1005009105267126.html).

  </td>
  <td>

![ptfe-collector.webp](/media/images/ptfe-collector.webp =640x){.align-center}

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

- **Silicone sleeve clamp:** Secures the tube from the interior to the exterior of the machine. The outer diameter of the silicone tube with the PTFE inside is around 5.3mm.
  - You can reuse the one installed by default.
  - Compatible replacements are available on [Aliexpress (White Hose Clamps 5~5.5mm)](https://aliexpress.com/item/1005005697827712.html).

  </td>
  <td>

![ptfe-hose-clamp.webp](/media/images/ptfe-hose-clamp.webp =640x){.align-center}

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

- **PTFE Tube:** This tube guides the filament from the spool to the extruder, so we need a tube with an internal diameter (2.5~3.0mm) slightly larger than what is used in the Bowden system (1.9~2.2mm).
  - [Replacement tube supplied by Artillery](https://artillery3d.com/products/ptfe-tube-m1)
  - [Capricorn replacement tube](https://www.captubes.com/shop/#!/Filament-Guide/c/173671010) (internal diameter 3, external diameter 4). The best PTFE tubes on the market.
  - [Mellow replacement tube](https://aliexpress.com/item/1005008268651680.html) (internal diameter 3, external diameter 4). Proven good quality.
  - [Mellow low-friction star-shaped replacement tube](https://aliexpress.com/item/1005010202912335.html) (internal diameter 2.2, external diameter 4). Although it has a Bowden-specific internal diameter, its star-shaped design provides less friction; however, **it is not recommended for TPU or abrasive materials** (the star design can create more friction than a conventional design with these filaments)[^1].

  </td>
  <td>

![ptfe-tube.webp](/media/images/ptfe-tube.webp =640x){.align-center}

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

- **PTFE Tube Disassembly Tool:** This tool allows us to remove the PTFE tube from the extruder.
  - Available on the printer's MicroSD card.
  - [STL of the tool.](/media/stl/official_disassembling_ptfe_tool.zip)

  </td>
  <td>

![ptfe-tool.webp](/media/images/ptfe-tool.webp =640x){.align-center}

  </td>
</tr>
</table>

## Guide

> Artillery provides a [tutorial on their Wiki](https://wiki.artillery3d.com/m1/maintenance/add-the-ptfe-connector-assembly) for tube replacement.
{.is-info}

### Preliminary Steps

Remove the filament before starting and disconnect the printer from the power source.

### PTFE Tube Removal

<table>
<tr>
  <th>Step</th>
  <th>Image (Click to Expand)</th>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

1. Remove the white clamp securing the sleeve on the outside of the printer.

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-1.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-1.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-1.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

2. Using the extraction tool, pull the tube out of the extruder.

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-2.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-2.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-2.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

3. Disconnect the connection clamps and remove the zip tie near the extruder (if still present).

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-3.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-3.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-3.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

4. Remove the clip from the one-way connector and pull it out from the extruder side.

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-4.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-4.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-4.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

5. Pull the PTFE tube from the inside out. The one-way connector at the end of the tube only allows removal from the extruder side, so **do not force it in the opposite direction** (it will damage the tube).

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-5.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-5.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-5.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
</table>

### PTFE Tube Installation

<table>
<tr>
  <td>

6. Insert the one-way connector that will go inside the printer. The area where the blue clip goes is closest to the printer wall.

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-6.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-6.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-6.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

7. Insert the PTFE tube into the extruder and adjust the tube length by moving the extruder across the bed.

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-7.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-7.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-7.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

8. Attach the connection clamps and secure the one-way connector with the blue clip.

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-8.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-8.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-8.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

9. Connect the white clamp to the silicone pin. **Do not overtighten the white clamp, just enough to keep the PTFE tube in place.**

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-9.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-9.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-9.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td>

10. Pass the PTFE tube through the final one-way connector.

  </td>
  <td>

<a href="/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-10.webp" target="_blank">![change-ptfe-tube-step-10.webp](/media/images/change-ptfe-tube-step-10.webp =640x){.align-center}</a>

  </td>
</tr>
</table>
